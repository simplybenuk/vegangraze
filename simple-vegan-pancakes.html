I never really ate pancakes. That was until I became vegan. Now they’re my weekend morning treat. This simple vegan pancake recipe is a doddle to make and is a great start to the weekend.

This recipe makes about 8 to 10 pancakes for stacking:

Ingredients

  • 200g plain flour
  • 2 tablespoons caster sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 300ml cold water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • More vegetable oil for frying

Method

  1. Into a mixing bowl put the flour, caster sugar, baking powder and salt and oil.
  2. Start to mix together until the oil is loosely mixed in (this won’t take long) then add in the water a little at a time, mixing each splash in fully before you add more.
  3. When you’ve mixed it all in that there are no visible lumps you’re batter is ready. I now normally pour the batter from the mixing bowl back into the measuring jug I used to measure out the water. This makes it easier to pour for the pancakes.
  4. Add a little vegetable oil to a frying pan on a medium heat. When hot, pour in some batter to make a pancake. The batter should sizzle as it hits the hot oil.
  5. Leave the batter to cook for about 2 minutes. The uncooked top should start getting little bubble holes in it when ready and more importantly not make a mess when you flip it. Then take a spatula, get fully underneath the pancake, lift and flip. Don’t worry if you make a mess, it’ll still be edible.
  6. Once done to your liking on both sides plate up. I personally keep them on a plate in the oven at about 40Â°C whilst I make the others.
  7. Repeat from step 4 until you’re out of batter (you may need to add a little oil for each pancake, I do as it makes it easier)
  8. Serve with your choice of fresh fruit, sugar, chocolate, syrup, lemon juice, sugar, etc etc then get back to bed as it’s the weekend!
